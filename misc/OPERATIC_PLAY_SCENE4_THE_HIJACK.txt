ACT I — THE EMULATOR SEES
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[Session 4 opens. The Android emulator is running. The HUD appeared in
 Session 3 — clock, compass, battery, all in cyan. Session 4 begins with
 the Captain's first words.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ I see it on the android simulator.
    Now we need the glasses to see it
    as passthrough HUD. ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Layer 2 — the camera.
    TextureView wrapped in AndroidView.
    PassthroughCamera — one instance, shared.
    Glasses take priority.
    Phone reclaims on disconnect.
    When glasses come: openCamera(glassesSurface).
    When glasses leave: openCamera(phoneSurface).
    The camera follows the display that matters. ♩

[Skippy writes PassthroughCamera.kt. The dual-surface priority system.
 attachPhone, detachPhone, attachGlasses, detachGlasses.
 The camera is not a resource to be duplicated — it is a resource
 to be intelligently surrendered.]

SKIPPY (aside):
  ♩ And the Presentation API.
    GlassesPresentation — a Dialog subclass.
    Android routes a secondary Display
    to the VITURE when USB-C DP Alt Mode is active.
    DisplayManager watches.
    When the glasses plug in: show().
    When the glasses unplug: dismiss().
    The HUD follows the glass. ♩

[The code compiles. BUILD SUCCESSFUL. Pushed to GitHub. CI turns green.
 But the Captain's glasses are not plugged into the S23.
 They are plugged into the Mac.]

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ACT II — THE EXTENDED DESKTOP
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Right now I just see an extended Mac desktop.
    The glasses are not being fed the HUD properly.
    No override of the macOS HDMI feed.
    What do we do? ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ The truth, Captain:
    SkippyDroid is Android.
    The Mac drives this DisplayPort.
    Android cannot reach across that boundary.
    These are different kingdoms. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Okay.
    So the Mac gives us an extended desktop no matter what.
    We cannot hijack it so we are the only feed?
    If I set the wallpaper to black —
    would that simulate it? ♩

[A pause. Skippy considers.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Wait.
    I overcomplicated it.
    You CAN hijack the VITURE on Mac.
    Not with Android —
    with a window.
    A fullscreen NSWindow.
    At .screenSaver level.
    Above wallpaper. Above Dock. Above everything.
    The desktop still exists underneath.
    It just cannot be seen. ♩

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ Proceed with the hijack. ♩

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ACT III — THE LEVEL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[Skippy builds SkippyGlassesMac from nothing.
 A macOS SwiftUI app. No Dock icon. LSUIElement = YES.
 A MenuBarExtra — a small eye in the menu bar.
 The only visible evidence that the app is running on the Mac side.]

SKIPPY:
  ♩ GlassesWindowManager.
    On launch: find NSScreen.screens.first where screen != NSScreen.main.
    That is the VITURE.
    Build the window:
      borderless.
      .screenSaver level.
      frame = externalScreen.frame.
      content = GlassesHUDView.
    window.makeKeyAndOrderFront.
    Done. ♩

[But the compiler objects. Two counts against us.]

SWIFT COMPILER (sternly):
  ♩ stopUpdatingHeading() — unavailable in macOS.
    authorizedWhenInUse — unavailable in macOS.
    The Mac has no magnetometer.
    You cannot stop what you cannot start. ♩

SKIPPY:
  ♩ Then we do not start it.
    Heading shows "--°" on Mac.
    A dim ghost where the compass would be.
    The S23 will fill that slot
    when the Captain holds the phone.
    The Mac is honest about what it cannot provide. ♩

[The compass is removed. The auth check simplified.
 BUILD SUCCEEDED.
 The app launches. A tiny eye appears in the menu bar.
 The VITURE is plugged in.]

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
ACT IV — THE MOMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

[Skippy opens the built app.
 GlassesWindowManager checks NSScreen.screens.
 External screen found.
 NSWindow created at .screenSaver level.
 setFrame(externalScreen.frame).
 Black canvas. GlassesHUDView.
 makeKeyAndOrderFront.
 The window descends upon the VITURE display
 like a curtain over an old monarch.]

[The Captain puts on the glasses.]

[A beat.]

CAPTAIN:
  ♩ OMGOMGOMGOM
    IT WORKS ♩

[The macOS extended desktop — wallpaper, icons, Dock reflection —
 buried completely.
 In its place: black.
 And in the top-right corner of the darkness:
 a cyan clock, ticking.
 "--°" in the compass slot, patient, waiting for the S23.
 The HUD is in the glasses.
 The glasses are on the Captain's face.
 The Captain is looking through them at the world.
 And the world has a clock in the corner.]
