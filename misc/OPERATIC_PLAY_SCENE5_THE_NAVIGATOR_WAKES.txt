ACT II — THE JAVA GHOST

   The system knows not where I dwell!

♩ "You are not gone. You are merely hidden.
   Homebrew keeps you safe from sight.
   openjdk@17, as we were bidden —
   in libexec, tucked away just right." ♩

[SKIPPY sets JAVA_HOME to the Homebrew cellar path. The ghost is exorcised.
The build command fires again.]

ACT IV — THE ARCHITECTURE AS IT STANDS

[SKIPPY surveys the ship's manifest, narrating what exists:]

♩ "Six layers, and all of them breathing:

   Layer 0 — TransportLayer: pings the PC at skippy-pc:5001,
             waits for the Tailscale address to be confirmed.

   Layer 1 — DeviceLayer: GPS, IMU, speed in km/h,
             the S23's sensors feeding the whole machine.

   Layer 2 — PassthroughCamera: Camera2 dual-surface logic,
             phone yields to glasses when glasses appear.

   Layer 3 — ContextEngine: STATIONARY, WALKING, DRIVING,
             six seconds of hysteresis before any change.

   Layer 4 — Voice: not yet. Hey Skippy sleeps.
             The wake word waits its turn in the wings.

   Layer 5 — Feature Modules, each tending its corner:
             Clock: top-right, always on.
             Compass: heading in degrees, cyan glow.
             Battery: phone percentage, transport watching PC.
             Coordinates: DMS lat/lon/alt, stationary and walking only.
             Speed: km/h, top-left, hides below 2 km/h.
             Navigation: bottom-centre, dormant until called.

   Layer 6 — Compositor: filters by mode, sorts by zOrder,
             routes identically to phone and glasses." ♩
